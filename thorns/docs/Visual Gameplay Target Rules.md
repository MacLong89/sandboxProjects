# THORNS — Gameplay Visual Target Rules

Design reference for world art, lighting, atmosphere, and environment density. **Gameplay readability comes first**; atmosphere supports gameplay, not the reverse.

---

## Art Direction Identity

### Short descriptions

**Readable cinematic low-poly survival.**

One-line internal art direction:

> A readable low-poly survival world with cinematic lighting, sparse natural composition, and subtle dreamlike atmosphere.

Even shorter:

> **Beautifully lonely low-poly survival.**

### What it blends

- Stylized realism
- Sparse atmospheric survival
- Grounded low poly
- Dreamlike wilderness
- Painterly lighting
- Clean PvP readability

Sits somewhere between the readability of **The Long Dark**, atmosphere of **Firewatch**, gameplay clarity of **Rust**, silhouette simplicity of **Valheim**, environmental calm of **Journey**, and stylized Unreal/Unity cinematic showcase lighting — with Thorns’s own **Bloom corruption** identity layered on top.

### Key characteristics

**1. Low poly geometry, realistic proportions**

The world is **not** exaggerated cartoony proportions, voxel/blocky, or chibi/stylized fantasy.

It **is** realistic scale, simplified geometry, large angular forms, and believable terrain. That grounded proportion is why it feels immersive.

**2. Sparse environmental composition**

Environments are intentionally uncluttered. Emptiness supports readability, loneliness, scale, mood, and visibility. Negative space is part of the style.

**3. Cinematic lighting without cinematic obstruction**

Lighting is soft, warm, atmospheric, directional, and painterly — but it does **not** hide players, blur terrain, destroy visibility, or overuse fog. This is one of the most important aspects of the whole aesthetic.

**4. Strong silhouette-driven art direction**

Trees, rocks, towers, cliffs, animals, players, and buildings read clearly at distance. Shapes do the heavy lifting, not texture detail.

**5. Soft restrained color palette**

Base world: dusty greens, warm tans, muted blues, soft browns, pale skies. Very little saturation spam.

The Bloom is a **controlled accent**: cyan/purple bioluminescence and visual contrast against grounded nature — so corruption feels special, not like another biome recolor.

**6. “Beautifully lonely”**

The world feels quiet, ancient, abandoned, calm, slightly sad, peaceful, and dangerous underneath — not noisy, crowded, theme-park-like, or over-designed.

**7. Gameplay-first atmosphere**

The look secretly serves gameplay: long-distance visibility, PvP, traversal readability, landmark recognition, tactical combat, and orientation — without looking “competitive.” That balance is rare.

## Core Pillars

- Gameplay readability comes first
- Atmosphere supports gameplay, not the reverse
- Low-poly but grounded
- Sparse but believable
- Beautifully lonely
- Dreamlike, not blurry
- Stylized realism, not cartoon fantasy

---

## World Readability

### Visibility

- Players must be clearly visible at gameplay engagement ranges
- Near/mid range visibility stays crisp and readable
- Atmosphere affects mostly far distance
- Never obscure gameplay clarity with heavy fog
- Terrain silhouettes must read instantly

### Terrain

- Large readable landforms
- Broad valleys, ridges, cliffs, rivers
- Minimal terrain noise / micro bumps
- Traversable-looking terrain
- Terrain should feel natural, not procedural-chaotic

### Environment Density

- Sparse environments
- Intentional placement
- Large areas of negative space
- Avoid clutter spam
- Foliage supports composition, not realism

---

## Lighting Rules

### General Lighting

- Clear daylight baseline
- Soft natural shadows
- Bright readable environments
- Minimal bloom
- No overexposed haze

### Time of Day

- Sunrise / sunset used selectively for atmosphere
- Midday remains readable and attractive
- Night should still preserve gameplay clarity

### Color

- Restrained earthy palette
- Muted greens
- Dusty blues
- Warm sunlight
- Soft cool shadows
- Limited saturation spikes

---

## Fog / Atmosphere

### Fog Usage

- Distant atmospheric haze only
- Never thick eye-level fog during normal gameplay
- Fog used to create scale and depth
- Atmosphere should separate terrain layers naturally

### Atmospheric Goals

- Calm
- Lonely
- Ancient
- Slightly wrong
- Quiet tension

---

## Low Poly Style Rules

### Geometry

- Strong silhouettes
- Large readable forms
- Simplified geometry
- Minimal tiny details
- Terrain and assets should read from distance

### Texture Style

- Soft / simple materials
- Avoid noisy textures
- Avoid photoreal texture detail
- Geometry carries the style more than textures

---

## Foliage Rules

### Trees

- Sparse placement
- Large silhouette variation
- Clear trunks / canopies
- Readable from distance

### Grass / Bushes

- Used sparingly
- Never dense enough to obscure players
- Supports biome identity without clutter

### Flowers / Detail

- Small tasteful accents
- Rare saturation pops
- Used to guide atmosphere / composition

---

## PvP Readability Rules

### Combat Clarity

- Terrain cover must be understandable instantly
- Sightlines should feel fair
- Players should pop against terrain
- Visual effects must never hide combat information

### Environment Design

- Terrain creates tactical gameplay
- Not foliage walls
- Not visibility suppression
- Shape language over visual noise

---

## The Bloom Rules

### Bloom Philosophy

**The Bloom is:**

- beautiful
- invasive
- unnatural
- bioluminescent
- seductive
- slowly consuming the world

**NOT:**

- generic corruption sludge
- edgy darkness
- total environmental replacement

### Bloom Visuals

- Cyan / purple / pink bioluminescence
- Soft glowing flora
- Vines
- Fungal growth
- Floating spores
- Subtle environmental takeover

### Bloom Usage

- Applied selectively
- Gradual spread
- Never fully overwhelms readability
- Used to contrast normal environments

---

## Town / Structure Rules

### Settlements

- Sparse
- Functional
- Survival-oriented
- Built from simple materials
- Integrated into terrain naturally

### Architecture

- Watchtowers
- Palisades
- Ruins
- Cabins
- Docks
- Desert outposts
- Small compounds

### Structure Style

- Low poly
- Clear silhouettes
- Readable layouts
- Minimal decoration
- Weathered but grounded

---

## Overall Emotional Goal

**The player should feel:**

- isolated
- vulnerable
- curious
- calm
- tense
- exploratory

**The world should feel:**

Beautiful, quiet, readable, and fundamentally wrong underneath.
